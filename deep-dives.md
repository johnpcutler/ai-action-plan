# Level Deep Dives

Individual COM-B behavioral diagnoses for each level of the AI Transformation Model. Each analysis examines both the adoption behavior (why organizations struggle to reach that level) and the progression blocker (why they get stuck and can't advance to the next).

---

## [Level 1: AI as a Thought Partner](level-1-thought-partner-com-b.md)

**Goal:** Help people explore ideas and improve decisions

**What blocks it:** Permission vacuum — nobody said it's okay. Senior professionals feel identity threat. Success is invisible.

**Key finding:** Social permission is the primary gate. Leader modeling closes the gap faster than any training program.

---

## [Level 2: AI as an Assistant](level-2-assistant-com-b.md)

**Goal:** Complete individual tasks faster, save employee time

**What blocks it:** Neutral-to-hostile environment — gains are invisible, incentives are misaligned, nobody owns integration.

**Key finding:** Incentive structure, identity conflict, and invisible gains are the silent killers. People won't sustain what isn't recognized.

---

## [Level 3: AI as Teammates](level-3-teammates-com-b.md)

**Goal:** Automate repetitive work, increase team efficiency

**What blocks it:** Structural exclusion — missing platforms, unclear ownership, premature governance, capability gaps for non-engineers.

**Key finding:** The jump from "using AI tools" to "building AI agents" is qualitative, not quantitative. Prompting skills don't transfer. Non-engineering teams are structurally excluded unless deliberately supported.

---

## [Level 4: AI as the System](level-4-system-com-b.md)

**Goal:** Run critical workflows, scale organizational capacity

**What blocks it:** Active immune response — political dynamics, power redistribution, entrenched incentives, accountability gaps.

**Key finding:** This is fundamentally an organizational design and political problem, not a technology problem. The organizational immune system fights back.

---

*Each deep dive runs the full [Change Lenses and Actions](https://github.com/johnpcutler/change-lenses-and-actions) diagnostic pipeline: behavior canvas, four-lens analysis, practitioner worksheet, intervention mapping, and phased recommendations.*
